Planets

Here is a gallery of the planets of our solar system. A short description is next to each of them.

Mercury

The planet closest to the Sun, warmed by its rays on one side and chilled by cosmic vacuum on the other. The lack of an atmosphere does not improve the situation on this hostile planet.

Venus

Named after the goddess of beauty, she is her name only. The conditions there would easily melt the lead and the enormous pressure crushed the organism residing there.

Earth

Our home. The only friendly place we know. The world is mostly covered with liquid water. The dense atmosphere and the developed magnetosphere protect the organisms living there.

Mars

A red planet that takes its color from the iron oxides on it. It may resemble the Earth's desert, but it is by no means hot there. The atmosphere is 100 times less dense than the Earth's atmosphere.

Jupiter

The largest planet of the solar system. The gas giant. Known for the huge storms raging in the upper atmosphere. Like the Great Red Spot, for example.

Saturn

Lord of the Rings. A gas giant with an extremely low density and the owner of a very numerous and interesting group of moons.

Uranium

A distant cool world with a blue color. Uranus appears to move on its side due to its highly tilted axis of rotation.

Neptun

The farthest planet. Very little known so far. Like Uranus, it is bluish-blue in color and consists mainly of hydrogen and methane. Highly elliptical orbit.

Planets discoveries

Discovery of Pluto

Year 1930

The discovery of this initially called the planet of the celestial body (today included in the dwarf planes) took place at the latest due to its huge distance from the Sun and small size. The discovery of Pluto and the accompanying moon Charon was made by Clyde Tombaugh

Discovery of Neptuna

Year 1846

The existence of Neptune has been suspected for years, incl. Due to the gravitational influence, however, the observation was not documented until 1846. Only Johann Gottfried Galle did this using a refractor at the Cambridge Observatory. Neptune was discovered as a point moving in front of the stars.

Observation of Jupiter through a telescope

Year 1610

Jupiter has been known to mankind since ancient times because it is one of the brightest objects in the sky. However, from "up close" Galileo saw him for the first time using the telescope. He also made several other discoveries.

Discovery of Saturn rings

Year 1658

Looking at Saturn and admiring its rings, Galileo thought they were two planets on either side of it, until Christiaan Huygens described the disc around the planet some decades later.

Discovery of the Galilean moons

Year 1610

Despite having not very powerful equipment for that time, Galileo saw four bright dots around Jupiter that turned out to be its moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto.
